## 1. Respect Other Players

> Treat other players, their builds, and their projects with respect. Griefing, stealing, harassment, unwanted destruction, or any action intended to negatively impact another player's experience is prohibited.

**Penalty:** Temporary ban. Repeat offenses may result in permanent removal.

---

## 2. Cheating & Exploits

> The use of cheats, hacked clients, x-ray resource packs, duplication exploits, or any modification intended to provide an unfair advantage is prohibited.

> Mods such as Freecam may be used for building purposes, but intentionally using them to locate resources, bases, hidden storage, or other player assets is considered cheating.

> Exploiting bugs, glitches, or unintended mechanics for personal gain is also prohibited.

**Penalty:** Permanent Ban

---

## 3. Spawn Area

> The spawn area serves as the central hub of Brassbound SMP. It contains starter resources, public facilities, and community projects designed to help new players begin their journey.

> Do not grief, hoard, steal from, or intentionally disrupt public infrastructure.

> Community resources should be shared fairly.

**Penalty:** Temporary ban. Repeat offenses may result in permanent removal.

---

## 4. Starter Settlements & Community Growth

During the early stages of a season, players are encouraged to establish starter bases, workshops, and shops within reasonable proximity of spawn.

This helps create a living, connected world where new players can interact with existing members and participate in the server economy.

Large-scale industrial complexes and mega-bases should be constructed outside the immediate spawn region and connected to the transportation network whenever possible.

---

## 5. Transportation Network

> Transportation is a core part of Brassbound SMP.

> Players are encouraged to connect long-term bases to the wider world using trains, conveyor systems, automated delivery networks, roads, or other transportation methods.

> The goal is to create a connected industrial civilization rather than isolated settlements.

> Staff may provide exemptions for temporary or early-game builds.

---

## 6. Currency & Trade

> Diamonds and Diamond Blocks are recognized as the primary server currency.

> Players are free to establish alternative trade agreements, barter systems, IOUs, contracts, or local currencies with mutual consent.

> Trade disputes may be reviewed by staff when evidence is available.

---

## 7. Offensive, Hateful, or NSFW Content

> Mods that allow image uploads, text displays, or custom content must not be used to display:

> * Hate speech
> * Racist or discriminatory content
> * Harassment or targeted abuse
> * Sexually explicit content
> * Graphic or disturbing imagery

**Penalty:** Permanent Ban

---

## 8. Player vs Player Combat

> Brassbound SMP operates under a mutual-consent PvP policy.

> PvP is allowed only when all involved players agree to participate.

> Repeatedly attacking, killing, or harassing players who do not wish to engage in PvP is prohibited.

**Penalty:** Temporary ban. Repeat offenses may result in permanent removal.

---

## 9. Building Standards

> You do not need to be an expert builder, but players are encouraged to make an effort to create structures that contribute positively to the world.

> Temporary starter structures are acceptable. Permanent builds should strive to fit the industrial and community-focused spirit of the server.

> If you need help building, ask the community. Collaboration is encouraged.

---

## 10. The End & The Dragon

> The Ender Dragon is a **protected entity** within Brassbound SMP.

> Intentionally killing the Ender Dragon is **prohibited**.

> Players may explore The End and obtain Elytra, Shulker Boxes, and other resources. However, all reasonable effort must be taken to avoid harming the dragon.

> Accidental dragon deaths will be reviewed by staff on a case-by-case basis.

> Intentional attempts to kill the dragon may result in penalties, including temporary or permanent restrictions from The End dimension.

---

## 11. Server Performance

> Create allows players to build extremely large machines, factories, trains, and automation systems.

> Any machine, farm, train, contraption, chunk-loading setup, or automated system that negatively impacts server performance may be modified, restricted, disabled, or removed by staff.

> Players may be asked to redesign systems that create excessive lag regardless of whether the system violates another rule.

> **Server stability always takes priority over automation efficiency.**

---

## 12. Land Ownership & Inactive Players

> Do not build directly adjacent to another player's base without permission.

> Bases belonging to players who have been inactive for extended periods may be reclaimed, repurposed, or removed at staff discretion.

> Staff will make reasonable efforts to contact inactive players before taking action.

> Public railways, roads, logistics hubs, canals, stations, and transportation systems are considered community infrastructure.

> Do not intentionally obstruct, reroute, remove, or interfere with established public infrastructure without permission from its owner or the community members responsible for maintaining it.

> If a conflict arises between a private project and existing infrastructure, players are expected to work together to find a reasonable solution.

---

## 13. Donations

> Donations are entirely voluntary and are used to support server operation, upgrades, maintenance, and future improvements.

> Donations do not grant ownership, administrative authority, special treatment, or guaranteed server availability.

> Donation rewards, if offered, may be adjusted for balancing or technical reasons.

## 14. Staff Authority

> Staff are expected to act fairly and consistently.

> If a situation arises that is not specifically covered by these rules, staff may make decisions based on the overall health of the server and community.

> Players may respectfully appeal staff decisions through designated community channels.

---

## 15. Rule Changes

> These rules may be updated as the server evolves.

> Changes may be made to address exploits, balance concerns, technical limitations, mod updates, or community needs.

> Continued participation on Brassbound SMP constitutes acceptance of the current version of these rules.

---

## 16. Logistics Network Naming

> Create's logistics systems rely on unique names and channels to function correctly.

> Players are **required** to place a unique server identifier on all shared logistics components, including but not limited to:

- Packagers
- Redstone Links
- Frog Ports
- Addresses
- Other channel-based Create devices

Players are **REQUIRED** to include a personal identifier for all your logistics.

**Recommended format:**

```
[PlayerName]_[SystemName]

Examples:
  Steve_IronFarm
  Steve_*
  Steve_Workshop_Press
```

> Using generic names such as `"Storage"`, `"Base"`, `"Train"`, `"Station"`, or similar identifiers may interfere with another player's systems and cause packages or signals to be routed incorrectly.

> Players are responsible for ensuring their identifiers remain unique.
